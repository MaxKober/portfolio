Port City Shopping & Entertainment Center

Retail • Design-Build GC • Altis • Lutsk, Ukraine

Design-Build • General Contracting Role: Project Manager Budget:$70M Size: 581,000 GSF Status: Completed ISO 9001

Overview

Client: Continuum Group Ukraine

Port City is a regional retail and entertainment complex in Lutsk, Ukraine, delivered under a Design-Build / General Contracting model. The project included large-format retail, boutique galleries, F&B, entertainment, and extensive BOH/service areas and was completed within the owner’s cost, schedule, and quality targets. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

As Project Manager I oversaw full-cycle delivery — from design coordination and tendering through site execution — managing an in-house team and consultants, aligning tenant requirements with base-building systems, and maintaining compliance with Ukrainian building codes and local by-laws. The project was executed within Altis’s ISO 9001 quality management framework, which ensured traceable documentation, disciplined change management, and structured handover. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Scope of Work

  • Design & Documentation: Coordination of architectural, structural, and MEP documentation for a multi-tenant retail/entertainment building, including delivery, storage, and back-of-house areas.
  • Retail & Tenant Interfaces: Alignment of base-building utilities (HVAC, electrical load, ELV, fire and life safety) with the technical requirements of anchor tenants, cinema/F&B, and smaller retail units.
  • Construction Management: Organization of site setup, phased interior works, envelope and roofing installation, and on-site QA/QC in accordance with ISO 9001 procedures.
  • MEP & Life Safety: Integration of fire protection, smoke extraction, sprinkler and alarm systems suitable for public assembly in a regional mall.
  • Procurement & Trades: Bid preparation, administration, and award of packages for façade, MEP, fit-out, and external works; supervision of key subcontractors and suppliers. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
  • Team Management: Led an in-house and field team of ~28 staff to deliver the project in line with cost, quality, and schedule benchmarks. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Challenge

Deliver a high-occupancy retail and leisure destination with multiple tenant types — each with different technical demands — while keeping base-building systems unified, code-compliant, and buildable, and doing so under a design-build GC model where the contractor carried both design coordination and construction risk. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Solution

Structured the project into base-building and tenant-interface packages; applied Altis ISO 9001 procedures to manage submittals, RFIs and design clarifications; and phased construction so that interior fit-outs could overlap with envelope/MEP completion. Close coordination with specialty consultants and equipment suppliers reduced conflicts in dense MEP and F&B areas and kept the opening strategy on track.

Result

Achieved full delivery of a 581,000 GSF regional shopping and entertainment center in Lutsk under the design-build GC model with controlled cost, schedule, and quality; tenant-ready base-building systems; and handover supported by ISO 9001-compliant documentation. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
