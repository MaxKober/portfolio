Nikolsky Mall, Kharkiv

Retail • EPC • Altis • Kharkiv, Ukraine

EPC Role: Project Manager Structural • Post-Tension BIM Coordination Completed

Overview

Client: Nikolsky Mall Development Group

Nikolsky Mall is one of the largest retail developments in Eastern Ukraine, delivering over 108,000 m² of commercial space across six floors with a 700-stall underground parking facility. As Project Manager, I led EPC delivery for the structural concrete frame implemented using post-tension slab technology.

Scope of Work

  • Full EPC delivery for the reinforced-concrete structural frame.
  • Integration of post-tension slab technology for long-span floor plates.
  • Coordination with Norwegian post-tension design consultants and technology supplier.
  • Execution of quality-control procedures, including marking all PT cable routes on slab undersides to prevent cable damage during anchoring, drilling, fastening, partition installation, and subsequent trades.

Challenge

Post-tension slab technology was new to both the local design teams and site crew, as well as to the GC. The learning curve, combined with extremely tight structural tolerances and a large building footprint, created substantial risk of cable strikes during follow-up construction activities.

Solution

A collaborative engineering workflow with Norwegian post-tension specialists has been organized to support QA/QC processes during installation process. To mitigate the high-risk issue of post-tension cable strikes, full-surface marking system using high-visibility paint on the underside of slabs to indicate tendon paths for all subcontractors has been performed.

Result

The structural frame was delivered to full compliance with no tendon-damage incidents. Nikolsky Mall structural frame was successfully completed.
