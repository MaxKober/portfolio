Overview

Alliance Privilege is a 54-storey (185 m) mixed-use tower on Batumi’s waterfront that combines a Marriott-branded hotel with luxury residential units, amenities, podium and underground parkade. The project is being delivered under a developer-led Design-Build / CMAR model.

I led the front-end setup of the project: defined which disciplines would be executed in-house and which required external consultants, and then coordinated all of them to keep brand standards, local code and constructability aligned.

External consultants I identified and coordinated included structural design, fire and life safety, façade CFD analysis, interior design, acoustical, vertical transportation, spa and pool technology, kitchen and restaurant technology, diesel generator and power infrastructure, grounding and lightning protection, and the power substation. Architectural and MEP design parts were delivered in-house under my supervision.

I prepared and administered RFPs, defined scopes for consultants, suppliers and contractors, and ran bid preparation and administration for the envelope, MEP and fit-out packages — keeping design intent and budget on the developer’s side.

Scope of Work

End-to-end design and preconstruction management for a mixed hotel/residential tower, podium, and parkade.

  • Defined and coordinated external consultants for structure, fire/life safety, façade CFD, interiors, acoustics, vertical transport, spa/pool, kitchen/restaurant technology, diesel generator, power substation, and lightning/grounding.
  • Delivered architectural and MEP design in-house and integrated them with external packages to maintain consistency and constructability.
  • Defined the steel structure consultant/subcontractor for steel portions of the tower and oversaw their design coordination and on-site implementation.
  • Prepared and administered RFPs; set scopes for consultants, suppliers and contractors; ran bid preparation for envelope, MEP and fit-out packages.
  • Coordinated shell and core delivery (structure, envelope, vertical circulation, risers) to support phased fit-out and hotel/residential stacks.
  • Planned city-network tie-ins (power, water, sewage, comms) together with generator, substation and protection systems.
  • Supported high-rise logistics, crane studies and sequencing to match the CMAR execution plan.
