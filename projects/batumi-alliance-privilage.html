Alliance Privilege Tower

Hospitality / Residential • DB + CMAR (Owner-led) • Batumi, Georgia

DB + CMAR Role: Project Manager Budget: ~CAD105M (USD$80M) Size: ~890,000 GSF Height: 54-storey Brand: Marriott Status: In Delivery (phase-based)

Overview

Client / Developer: Alliance Group (Batumi)

A flagship, mixed-use high-rise in the heart of Georgia’s Black Sea resort city. The tower integrates a Marriott-branded hotel with residential and amenity programs under a Design-Build / CMAR model. I directed full-cycle coordination across design development, procurement, and construction execution on a fast-tracked, high-rise delivery.

Scope of Work

  • Design & Documentation: Coordinated multi-disciplinary teams (architecture, structure, MEP, interiors, vertical transportation). Ensured alignment between brand standards (Marriott), local codes, and constructability constraints for a 54-storey tower.
  • Preconstruction & Methodology: Packages scoping, bid strategy, logistics and crane studies for waterfront constraints, tower core sequencing, and high-rise safety/QA plans.
  • Construction Management: Site establishment, vertical concrete works, envelope interfaces, tower MEP risers, podium-to-tower handoffs, and high-finish hotel/residential fit-outs.
  • Commercial & Controls: Budget ownership under DB/CMAR hybrid, trade procurement, CR/CO management, and earned-value tracking to protect schedule and margin.
  • Stakeholders: Owner/developer, brand operator, consultants, AHJ/permits, and trade partners across structural, façade, MEPF, and interiors scopes.

Challenges

Delivering a 54-storey mixed-use tower with brand-standard hotel product in a coastal microclimate, coordinating international specifications with local code requirements, and maintaining schedule certainty through vertical logistics and high-rise safety controls.

Solutions

Implemented phased procurement aligned to core-and-shell milestones; established strict interface matrices for envelope/MEP; used clash-resolution and pull-planning to lock critical path; and standardized finish packages for hotel/residential stacks.

Results (to date)

Secured trade coverage at target values, maintained tower critical-path continuity, and safeguarded quality metrics for brand handover criteria while balancing budget, schedule, and procurement risk in a dynamic market.
