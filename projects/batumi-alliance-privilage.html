Alliance Privilege Tower

Hospitality / Residential • DB + CMAR (Owner-led) • Batumi, Georgia

DB + CMAR Role: Project Manager Budget: ~CAD105M (USD$80M) Size: ~890,000 GSF Height: 54-storey (185 m) Brand: Marriott Status: Completed (residential part)

Overview

Client / Developer: Alliance Group (Batumi)

A flagship 54-storey mixed-use tower on Batumi’s waterfront combining a Marriott-branded hotel with luxury residences, podium amenities, and underground parking. Delivered under a Design-Build / CMAR hybrid model, the project integrates international brand standards, high-rise engineering, and local regulatory compliance within a constrained coastal site.

I oversaw the full preconstruction and design-coordination cycle — leading in-house and external design teams, managing procurement strategy, and ensuring constructability and cost alignment through every delivery stage.

All design disciplines were managed under my coordination, with external consultants engaged for structural design, fire and life safety, façade CFD analysis, interior design, acoustics, vertical transportation, spa and pool technology, kitchen technology, food and beverage (restaurant) planning, diesel generator and power infrastructure, grounding and lightning protection, and the power substation. The architectural and MEP design were executed by the in-house team, ensuring seamless integration with external packages and full compliance with brand standards and local regulations.

Scope of Work

  • Design & Documentation: Directed design delivery for the tower, hotel podium, and two-level parkade including BOH areas and public spaces.
  • Coordination: Managed multidisciplinary interfaces among architecture, structure, MEP, façade, vertical transport, signage, and city networks.
  • Envelope & Fit-outs: Administered design development and bid packages for façade systems, interior finishes, and building services.
  • Procurement & Value Engineering: Led RFP preparation, bid evaluation, and value engineering to optimize envelope, MEP, and fit-out costs.
  • Shell & Core: Oversaw structure, envelope, risers, and vertical logistics to enable phased completion and brand-standard fit-outs.
  • Construction Methodology: Supported high-rise sequencing, crane logistics, safety, and QA plans aligned with CMAR execution.

Challenge

Deliver a 54-storey (185 m) mixed-use tower in a coastal microclimate while coordinating international brand requirements (Marriott), local Georgian regulatory frameworks, and multiple specialty systems — all under a developer-led DB + CMAR structure that required clear interfaces between in-house design and outsourced consultants.

Solution

Broke the project into discipline-specific consultant packages, established interface matrices for envelope, MEP, vertical transport and amenity zones, synchronized procurement with core-and-shell milestones, and used bid administration plus value engineering to keep façade, MEP and fit-out trades within target values. High-rise logistics and crane studies were developed in parallel with design to maintain schedule certainty.

Result

Achieved integrated design between in-house architecture/MEP and all external specialties; secured consultant and trade coverage at target budgets; and maintained continuity of the tower delivery sequence for brand-compliant fit-outs and future phased handover.
